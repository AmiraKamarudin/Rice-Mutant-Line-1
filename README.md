## Background
This repository stores the supplementary materials and Shell scripts used to analyse whole-genome sequencing data obtained from Illumina NovaSeq 6000. The sequencing data was quality assessed with FastQC v0.12.1, trimmed with Trimmomatic v0.39, read mapped with BWA MEM v0.7.17, read deduplicated with samtools markdup v1.19.2, variant called with bcftools mpileup v1.19, and functionally annotated with SnpEff v5.2.1.

## Abstract
The use of induced gamma irradiation on a widely cultivated commercial rice variety in Peninsular Malaysia, MR297, has led to the emergence of a promising mutant line, ML-1 that exhibits high yield potential and enhanced resistance to bacterial leaf blight disease. To characterise the improvements imposed to the genetic makeup of this mutant, we sequenced the mutant genome on Illumina NovaSeq 6000 at 40 x depth and generated 14.39 Gbp of raw nucleotides consisting of 95.95 million paired-end reads with average insert sizes of 150bp. These reads were cleaned, trimmed, aligned to a reference genome (AGIS1.0 genome assembly) and deduplicated. We discovered a total of 4,013,384 variations across the genome post-mapping where 90.85% of them are single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) and 9.15% are insertion-deletion mutations (InDels). Functional annotation analysis has annotated 10.9 million of variant effects and classified them according to their impact levels. Sequencing data are publicly available at the NCBI Sequence Read Archive repository under the accession number SRR37260596. This data aids comparative genomics studies by providing insights into the genomic architecture of a mutagenised rice variety that will support future research in mutation breeding.
